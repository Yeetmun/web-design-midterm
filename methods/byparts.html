Intro

Integration by parts, also called the Reverse Product Rule is a method to find the integral for certain integrals that may prove tricky to solve with other methods. It is often helpful when there are two functions multiplied together. The general form of integration by parts is as follows ∫f(x)g'(x)dx=f(x)g(x)-∫f'(x)g(x)dx


Example

Integration by parts is complicated and difficult to explain, so let's look directly at an example. Next, let's assign our functions, we want to assign f(x) so that its derivative is simpler that itself and assign g'(x) so that the integral of it is not more complicated than before. In this case f(x)=x and g'(x)=cos(x).
